About Me

I am a Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) developer and engineer with 8+ years of industry and academic experience. My work focuses on implementing advanced numerical methods, end-to-end simulation workflows, and parallel computing solutions for high-performance engineering applications.

Currently, I'm completing my Ph.D. in Aerospace Science and Technology at UPC-Barcelona, specializing in active aerodynamics flow control and high-fidelity CFD, while working as a Project Engineer at Applus+ IDIADA.

  • HPC Tools: Linux, Git, MPI, OpenMP, Paraview

Research Focus

Vortex Particle Method Development

Led the development of a vortex particle method code for automotive aerodynamics. Implemented efficient data structures and hybrid OpenMP/MPI parallelization.

Key achievements:

  • Enhanced boundary layer resolution with second-order accurate finite volume solver
  • Validated for canonical flows and industry-relevant geometries
  • Created detailed technology roadmap to advance code from TRL 3 to 6
Learn More

Physics-Informed Machine Learning for CFD

Led technical development in EU H2020 funded UPSCALE project related to implementation of physics-informed ML for turbulence modeling. Validated end-to-end workflows by coupling with OpenFOAM RANS solvers.

Developed real-time aerodynamic optimization framework using ML-enhanced CFD, reducing design evaluation time from 6-12 hours to 10 seconds, while maintaining solution accuracy within 2-5%.

Learn More

Active Flow Control Technologies

Developed novel open-source CFD framework in OpenFOAM for modeling synthetic jet actuators and flow control systems, enabling rapid evaluation of conceptual designs.

Conducted large-scale high-fidelity transient simulations of steady and periodic flow control jets on HPC infrastructure, analyzing complex flow physics and actuator-flow interactions.
